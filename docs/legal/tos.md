## 1. Service description

WebSec0 is a **passive** web security scanner that performs HTTP/TLS requests and DNS
lookups to audit a domain's public security configuration. It does not crawl, fuzz
parameters, exploit vulnerabilities, or authenticate to the target. All checks are
described in the [check catalog](/checks/). Scans are limited to ≤ 50 HTTP requests
per target, spread over 30–60 seconds.

## 2. Acceptable use

You may use WebSec0 only if **all** of the following apply simultaneously:

1. You own the target domain, or hold a written authorisation from its owner.
2. The target is a public web property reachable from the internet (not internal infrastructure, staging environments, or administrative back-ends not intended for public access).
3. Your use complies with all applicable laws, including but not limited to: the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA, US), the Computer Misuse Act 1990 (CMA, UK), §202c StGB (DE), and art. 323-1 du Code pénal (FR).
4. You are not acting on behalf of, or at the direction of, a state-level threat actor or an entity designated under applicable sanctions programmes.

Acceptable use also explicitly includes: security research on sites for which the operator runs a public bug bounty programme; testing one's own infrastructure; and educational use against explicitly authorised demonstration environments such as `badssl.com`.

## 3. Prohibited targets

Scanning the following is prohibited and will be refused by the public instance:

- Government and military domains: `.gov`, `.mil`, `.gouv.fr`, `.gc.ca`, `.gov.uk`, `.bund.de`, `europa.eu`, and equivalent TLDs and second-level domains.
- Critical infrastructure: energy, healthcare, banking, and financial services.
- Private IP ranges, loopback (`127.0.0.0/8`), link-local, and CGNAT (`100.64.0.0/10`).
- Any target whose `robots.txt` explicitly disallows the `WebSec0` User-Agent.

Attempts to scan prohibited targets will receive an HTTP `422` (private IP range) or HTTP `451` (domain blocklist) response.

## 4. Passive nature of the scanner and identifiable User-Agent

WebSec0 sends only standard HTTP/HTTPS requests and DNS queries. It does not inject payloads, attempt authentication, or perform port scanning beyond 443 (HTTPS) and the email ports listed in the check catalog. Every request carries the User-Agent:

```
WebSec0/{version} (+https://websec0.example/about; passive-scan)
```

The `/about` page explains how to block this scanner via `robots.txt` or firewall rules. Operators who do not wish to be scanned may disallow this User-Agent; WebSec0 respects explicit disallow rules.

## 6. Scan report management

- Reports are identified by a GUIDv4 (122 bits entropy) and are **not** listed publicly.
- Default retention on the public instance: **24 hours**, after which reports are automatically deleted.
- **Private mode**: scan requests with `options.private: true` return a 256-bit token required for subsequent access. The token is returned once and not stored by the server.
- Deletion on request: send the scan GUID to `privacy@websec0.example`.
- Reports are published under [Creative Commons BY 4.0](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/), meaning you may share and adapt them with attribution.
